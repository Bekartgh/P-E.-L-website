Navigating Ghana’s Industrial Fuel Challenges

In the tropical climate of Ghana, petroleum storage presents unique challenges. High ambient temperatures increase evaporation rates and pressure build-up. We examine how Possible Energy's strategic infrastructure mitigates these risks.

Technical Site Audit: Thermal Regulation

Thermal Expansion & Loss: Ghana's average daytime temperature can lead to significant thermal expansion in surface-level storage tanks. Without high-reflectivity coatings and precision pressure-vacuum vents, industrial operators can lose up to 1.5% of their bulk diesel stock to evaporation alone.

Possible Energy's Solution: We utilize dual-wall underground reservoirs and shaded surface-mounted systems that maintain a stable core temperature. This stabilization preserves the fuel's chemical integrity and prevents the sludge buildup common in overheated tanks.

Contamination Control: High humidity environments are breeding grounds for microbial growth in diesel. Our strategic analysis shows that a "seal-and-circulate" filtration system reduces engine injector failure by 40% over a 12-month period in mining environments.
